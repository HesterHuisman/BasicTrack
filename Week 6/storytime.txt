Kyle tossed the papers into the trash. "Don't need these anymore," he thought to himself. He thought the resume had been solid; after all, it got him his internship, unpaid as it was. That was the third rejection this week, and he had no great hopes for the rest of the week. Clearly he had to revamp something. Another failure. One more, he thought bitterly. He plucked the hand-addressed envelope out from the clutter of flyers and student loan bills, opened it, and read it quickly. Kyle grinned from ear to ear.
